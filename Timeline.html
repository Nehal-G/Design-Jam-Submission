THE ROLLERCOASTER LIFE

TIMELINE

January 8, 1942

Stephen Hawking was born in Oxford, England, and was the eldest of the four children of Frank Hawking, a biologist, and Isobel Hawking, a medical research secretary.

1952

In St Albans, the eight-year-old Hawking attended St Albans High School for Girls for a few months. At that time, younger boys could attend one of the houses.

1959

Stephen recieves a scholarship to attend University College, Oxford, from which he graduates with a degree in natural science.

1962

He begins his graduate research in cosmology at Trinity Hall, cambridge.

1963

Hawking is Diagnosed with the degenerative nerve disorder ALS, or Lou Gehrig's disease, at the age of 21. Hawking fell into a depression – though his doctors advised that he continue with his studies, he felt there was little point as he was given only two years to live.

1966

He completes his doctorate and is awarded a fellowship at Gonville and Caius College, Cambridge. He works on singularities in the theory of general relativity and applies his ideas to the study of black holes.

1970

In 1970, Hawking postulated what came to be known as the second law of black hole dynamics, that the event horizon of a black hole can never get smaller. He discovers a remarkable property that by using quantum theory and general relativity he is able to show that black holes can emit radiation.

1973

He joins the department of applied mathematics and theoretical physics at Cambridge. This very year he discovers, to his disbelief, that black holes could leak energy and particles into space, and even explode in a fountain of high-energy sparks.

1974

He is elected as a fellow of the Royal Society at age 32, one of the youngest people to receive the honour.

1977

Hawking is appointed as professor of gravitational physics at Cambridge.

1979

Becomes Lucasian Professor of Mathematics at Cambridge, a prestigious position once held by Isaac Newton. Hawking holds the post until 2009.

1982

Hawking was made a Commander of the Order of the British Empire (CBE) in 1982

1985

Admitted to a hospital in Geneva with pneumonia. He survives after an operation, but loses what remained of his speech. The next year he begins communicating through an electronic voice synthesizer that gives him his trademark robotic "voice."

1988

Hawking publishes A Brief History of Time: From the Big Bang to Black Holes, a classic introduction to today's most important scientific ideas about the cosmos. Recorded in the 1998 Guinness Book of Records as an all-time bestseller.

1989

Hawking was made a Companion of Honor by Queen Elizabeth II.

2001

Releases Universe in a Nutshell in the UK, a book that unravels the mysteries of recent breakthroughs in physics.

2002

Hawking releases On the Shoulders of Giants, The Great Works of Physics and Astronomy, an exploration of some of the greatest visionaries in the history of science including Copernicus, Kepler, Galileo, Newton and Einstein. He also publishes The Theory of Everything: The Origin and Fate of the Universe, a book that presents the most complex theories of physics past and present.

2004

Hawking announces that he has solved the Black Hole paradox, which has been a troubling scientists for years. He presents his most recent findings at the international conference on general relativity and gravitation in Dublin.

2007

Hawking and his daughter Lucy published George's Secret Key to the Universe, a children's book designed to explain theoretical physics in an accessible fashion and featuring characters similar to those in the Hawking family.

2017

Hawking was awarded an Honorary Doctorate from Imperial College London.

14 March 2018

Hawking died peacefull at his home in Cambridge, at the age of 76.
